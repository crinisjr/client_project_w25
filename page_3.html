Life Outside Classroom

Life outside the classroom at the University of Michigan is a vital part of the student experience, with diverse housing and dining options that foster a supportive and engaging community. The University offers various residence halls and off-campus housing to suit different lifestyles, ensuring all students find a comfortable home. Numerous dining facilities, from dining halls to cafes and food trucks, provide a wide array of cuisines to meet different dietary needs and preferences. These amenities not only address basic needs but also enhance the university journey, offering opportunities for social interaction and personal growth.

Maize and Blue Cupboard

The Maize and Blue Cupboard at the University of Michigan is a student-led initiative dedicated to combating food insecurity by providing free access to nutritious food and essential goods for university community members in need.

Housing

There are many options both for off and on campus housing:

Off Campus Housing

Private Apartments

Independence with full amenities in privately owned apartments near campus.

Rental Homes

More space and flexibility, ideal for groups of students renting together.

Co-op Housing

Affordable housing with shared responsibilities in a community setting.

On Campus Housing

Residence Halls

Traditional dormitory-style living with shared spaces and a strong community environment.

Suite-Style Living

More privacy with shared suites, offering a balance between community and independence.

University-Owned Apartments

Fully equipped apartments for students seeking independent living on campus.
